Map Information Welcome to World of Warcraft Reforged. This map is an open world RPG. This means that you can do whatever you want to. Complete some of the optional quests, hunt other players or forge alliances. Baradé wishes you a lot of fun while playing. Known Issues Unfortunately, this map has some known unfixed issues. If you think you know the cause of an issue or the solution to fix it, please report it on Discord.
Game Type In the beginning of the game, the game type is chosen. It determines certain aspects of the game and helps to match the game your desires.
Game Modes You can choose one of two game modes after the hero selection. As a warlord you play partly in the strategy mode and can build a base. This has the advantage that you have mostly a bigger army than other players. On the other hand, as a freelancer your hero starts with +15 points on all attributes and your hero gets 30% more experience from killing hostile units. Freelancers can see neutral buildings from start.
Heroes You have to pick a hero in the beginning of the game which can be repicked. You are allowed to pick two additional heroes at some point when you reached a higher level with your first hero.
Professions After choosing your race or game mode, you have to select a profession. The profession allows you to craft items. These items might help your hero or units to become stronger or to restore their stats or to fortify your base. Crafting items costs mana.
Races This selection is restricted to warlords. They receive a scepter and tiny town hall of their race which allows them to summon two workers and therefore to build their first base.
Start Locations The start locations allow you to start the game at some specified area on the map. Some start locations may be occupied by other players including Warlord AI. The Clan Island is restricted to clan members.
Victory If you voted for it in the beginning, every player can win the game by killing the Outland boss. The game ends after this. All players who are not allied with the killing player will lose the game.

Repick You can select your hero again by using the chat command "-repick". He keeps the gained experience but you lose all units on the map! All of your carried items will be dropped on the island Theramore, so quest items and rewards won't get lost. Repicks are allowed only every 30 seconds. You cannot rechoose your initially selected game mode.
Full Repick You can select your hero again including the game mode by using the chat command "-fullrepick". He keeps only a part of the gained experience and you lose all units on the map! All of your carried items will be dropped on the island Theramore, so quest items and rewards won't get lost. Repicks are allowed only every 30 seconds. You can reselect your initial game mode.
Alliances Alliances can be changed via the default Warcraft menu. You can also send resources via the standard menu. There are chat commands for single player games to change the alliances. The AI should react to your alliance changes. When you ally an AI player, the player should ally you as well etc. Special AI players or bosses are not included.
Hero Death If your hero dies you can either revive him/her in your own built altar if you are a warlord or revive him/her in the altar on Theramore or in your hideout. You can leave the island by buying a boat. If you have no gold, just sell crafted items. Note that ressurecting your hero on Theramore takes longer than in your own altar or hideout. Resurrection Stones allow reviving your hero automatically.

Cities and Hideouts Every warlord-type Hero has a nation-specific scepter. With this he or she can summon workers who will start building their nations. It is recommended that they build within city walls because the probability of their buildings being attacked by creeps or "terrorist" Heros is considerably lowered. Also, they can spawn citizens and build housings for them. Citizens make your town more realistic and also have some useful abilities. Hideouts are for Freelancer Heros. They are their personal homes and provide among other things a safe place to recover. It should be considered building them within city walls or under the protection of a warlord player to lower destruction probability.

Computer AI Computer players are warlords by default. They start with a main building, five workers and their hero. The AI will collect gold and lumber and build additional gold mines. It should attack creeps and hostile players from time to time. If you are allied to a computer player it is recommended to defend him and to give him resources if you are a warlord yourself. Allied computer players might turn out to be rather useful when attacking another player. When the base of a computer player is destroyed, the hero will get a tiny main building to rebuild a base. Their race is defined by their race settings in the lobby. Every Warcraft race is mapped to the races in this map with some randomized choosing.

Creeps You can kill creeps to increase the level of your hero. There is different creeps everywhere in the world. When you kill a group of computer-controlled creeps, it will be respawned after 100 seconds, so the number of opponents in the game is not finite. Some groups leave items after their extermination which can be picked up by your hero. The creeps won't respawn if there is a building in the area. When the building is destroyed, the creeps will respawn again.
Bosses Bosses are marked by the red skulls on the minimap. They are creep heroes which you can kill to gain experience and their legendary item. Each legendary item is unique in the game and has some powerful abilities. The legendary items are reset to their boss location when your first hero dies even if it is placed in your backpack or equipment bag.
Legendary Artifacts Legends and rumors speak of many legendary Artifacts hidden across the World. The bad news is, you can´t get them for free, but in order to retrieve them, must defeat each Dungeon Master guarding them. Since the Artifact Guardians are very powerful, it is advised not to venture alone into their lairs, or at least not well prepared. If a Hero carrying a unique artifact DIES without an Ankh or the Reincarnation skill, the artifacts will be removed from the Hero. They can then again be retrieved from the location where they were originally found.
Quests You can solve optional quests to get items as rewards. You recognize NPCs who give quests by the exclamation mark over their heads. If one player accepts a quest, he/she or any other player can solve the quest and gets a reward from the NPC for it. Use the chat command "-ping" to ping the current quest locations on the minimap.
Backpack You can select your backpack at the left top corner directly under your hero icon, so your hero can carry enough items with him/her. Use the two abilities in the backpack to change the current bag.
Equipment Bags Equipment bags allow your hero to equip additional items and to skill additional spells.

Demigod If you are bored with the standard heroes and you want to become more powerful, you can become a demigod. You can do this by buying either a 'Order Stone Fragment' (light side) or a 'Chaos Stone Fragment' (dark side) and by placing your hero in the center of the middle island above the Maelstorm, You have to place a level 10 dragon of the corresponding color in each fire. If your hero uses the item in the center of the island he/she will become a demigod automatically. The hero keeps the gained experience.
Mounts When your hero gets to level 20 he/she can cast an ability which summons a flying mount. This mount can transport one unit. Your hero can use it to fly over the Ocean or cliffs to get wherever he/she wants to. Your mount will be different depending on the race of your hero.

Hand of God When your hero gets to level 35 you can use the Hand of God somewhere on the map. It will summon creatures and damage hostile units.
Evolution You can research Evolution to increase the the maximum life, damage, armour and level of all of your units, so they will give enemy players more XP. The upgrade is also used for all creeps and units from bosses on the map as soon as the hero of playing users with the lowest hero level hits level 10. The upgrade is useful to continue leveling in late game and to kill strong heroes with a stronger army of units if you invested into units more than leveling.
Bonus Heroes Bonus heroes are additional heroes available at a higher hero level. Some of them are characters from Warcraft lore and have quite interesting hero abilities.
Goblin Bank The Goblin Bank is a neutral building located Theramore. You can use it to increase your gold and lumber by depositing it for some time and getting interests. You can also play the Wheel of Fortune there.
Clans You can either create or join a clan. Clans are groups of players who want to play together. Clans have members, a name, one leader, several captains and store gold and lumber for all members. Clans can have one Clan Hall in a game. Their stats will be stored in savecodes which can be improved even when not all clan members are playing.
VIPs VIPs are allowed to enter the VIP room and the secret chamber from the beginning. It provides them some bonuses in the game which should not be too unfair but still give them a bit of advantage or at least a visible status in the game. VIPs are usually players who helped to improve the map over time.
  • "-vips": Lists all VIPs.
Bans Accounts can be banned from the map. Players with banned accounts will be kicked automatically from the game in the beginning. Contact the map creator to get unbanned.
  • "-bans": Lists all banned account names.
Hall of Fame You will get to the Hall of Fame with a high hero level when you send your savecode on Discord (Multiplayer only) or when you complete the fastest Speed Run of the map: Killing Archimonde without cheats, using an official release version of the map, starting as the only player in the game and with hero level 1, without AI, with standard settings (Normal difficulty) and proof the whole game with a video. Of couse we cannot be sure that you did not use any cheat engine in the background but it would be pretty obvious during the gameplay. A replay might be fine as well as long as we can watch it in later Warcraft versions and it was played with one of the official release of the maps which are still available.
Rewarding Freelancers Warlords can reward freelancers with an ability in their main building. This will give a freelancer 800 gold, 800 lumber and 200 experience. It can be done every 180 seconds and helps to push freelancers.
Giving Units to an Ally Every player can give units to an allied player with an ability in his/her base or hideout. After using the ability on the given units you have to enter "-give playernumber" to give the units to a certain player. You can enter "-nogive" to cancel the process or simply cast the ability again.
Stonemaul Gauntlet The Stonemaul Ogres are famous for their passion for combat and fighting games. Their new warchief Rexxar has inspired them to establish a public gladiator arena. Here Heros can choose between different opponents to fight against in order to gain experience and maybe some small rewards. However, the Arena must first be officially opened by a certain Orc Quest. If you choose to fight in the arena, there is no turning back. If you enter the Arena with more than 1 Hero (Hero group), you lose the contest as soon as the 1st Hero dies. And if a Hero leaves the Arena before the fight is over, the battle is also over and you prove your cowardness to the Stonemaul Ogres. Aaahahahaharrr!
Races The Goblins on Kezan provide races which let the winners earn valuable cups. You have to buy a qualification to start a race. There can be only one race at a time. The first three winners will get a cup which can be sold. You can only participate with your first hero. Move your hero to all check points of the race in the correct order in the given time to win it.
  • Buy a qualification on Kezan to start.
  • Participate with your first hero.
Railway The Gnomish railway moves around Azeroth on the sea and land tracks. It allows any unit to enter or leave the train at railway stations which are around Azeroth. Units in the train are protected. The train can move in both directions.
Marketplaces Marketplaces sell items dropped by creeps.
Thieve Guilds Thieves Guilds sell units of enemy killed unit types. If you kill creeps or enemy units, your Thieve Guild will sell the unit. It is limited to only 1 to keep it balanced.
Trading Posts Trading Posts allow collecting ressources with trained Pack Horses. The Pack Horses search for an allied town hall furthest away and transport gold and lumber from the allied Trading Post furthest away to the town hall furthest away. The amount depends on the time it takes for the horses to reach the town hall and is increased with the time passing. The gold is also affected by taxes.
Properties Properties are unique indestructible buildings at fixed locations which can be purchased by one single player during the game. They allow hiring race-specific units and gathering resources by loading workers.
  • "-pingproperties": Pings all properties.
  • Unique places.
  • Hire units from one race.
  • Gather one specific resource by loading workers.
  • Produce food.
Random Events When your hero gets to level 10 you can purchase Random Events in your Power Generator which will happen somewhere on the map. After purchasing a random event you will have to wait for some time to be able to purchase another random event. This prevents spamming countless units on the map.
Hidden Bases Hidden bases are special locations which won't be reached by AI and can be used by Warlords to start a new base. They become available at hero level 35 but you can already go there before but not construct any buildings.
Player Stats There is a multiboard at the top right of the screen with the title "Stats" to show player stats of all players. It might help you to identify strong/weak players and compare yourself to other players. The counter in the header shows how many human players are still online from the beginning of the game. It does not include AI players or unused slots. It also shows the elapsed time of the game. Every row has the color of the player to make it easier to find the values of a certain player.
Special Race Buildings Every race has a special building which can be researched in the Tier 3 town hall and built by the Male Citizen. It can only be built once and has some special abilities for the race which have their origin in the lore of Warcraft III.
Save Codes You can save/load your hero settings and XP. However, it is restricted to the corresponding game type since the game type influences how fast you gain XP etc. Just make a screenshot of the savecode to store it on your disk.
  • "-savecodeconverter": Allows you converting old savecodes into new ones.
  • "-saveautoon/off": Enables/disables auto saving of your hero.
  • "-savegui": Shows the GUI for savecodes.
  • "-asave": Saves all savecodes in the auto load file.
  • "-aload": Loads all save codes from the auto load file.
  • "-areset": Allows saving and loading automatic save codes.
  • "-loadr X": Loads your researches from savecode X.
  • "-loadres X": Loads your resources from savecode X.
Portals The whole map contains many portals connecting places with each other. This is required to allow the AI to get everywhere easily without buying Zeppelins but also to connect all players with each other. No player should have an unfair advantage of reaching places much faster than others. Portals can also be built by Citizens to connect your different bases with each other. You won't have to cross the whole map with your army to defend your other bases. A research allows your built portals to teleport your units to any discovered place on the map except it is protected by an Antimagic Ward.
Crafting Stashes Crafting Stashes can be built by male citizens and allow you to craft items and summon creatures given the required items. Put the required items in the inventory and craft or summon as many items or creatures as you want to. Link Crafting Stashes with each other, to use multiple inventories for required items.
Mounts Cages Mounts Cages allow your hero to change his/her mount. Different mounts have different abilities, movement speeds and types.
Dragon Roosts Dragon Roosts allow you to train all different types of dragons.
Antimagic Wards Antimagic Wards can be built by female citizens. They block the enemy sight for the area around them. They also block enemy teleportation spells for this area and allow silencing enemy units.
Spell Books Spell Books can be built by female citizens. They contain all learnable hero spells. Only reskillable heroes can learn them.
Alchemist Labs Alchemist Labs can be built by female citizens. They allow converting units into different races.
Hero Abilities Hero Abilities can be built by female citizens. They your heroes to change their standard abilities.
Witch Huts Witch Huts can be built by female citizens. They allow giving units different abilities.
Armory Armories can be built to buy equipment items for customizable heroes.
Void Lord You can pick the Void Lord as hero when your main hero has reached the maximum hero level. The Void Lord can reskill all of his hero spells at the Arcane Academy of Theramore. Every spell slot can have one of several given hero spells. Once you have learned a hero spell, you can reset all hero spells to reskill again. There is a version of this special hero for every primary attribute. The hero causes Chaos damage and can walk on water.
Customizable Heroes Customizable heroes can change their hero attributes, spells and skins.
  • "-helpattributes": Lists attributes chat commands.
  • "-str X": Skills X strength. Skills max strength if empty.
  • "-agi X": Skills X agility. Skills max agility if empty.
  • "-int X": Skills X intelligence. Skills max intelligence if empty.
  • "-reseta": Resets attributes.
  • "-equala": Equally skilled attributes.
  • "-rename X": Renames selected heroes into X.
  • Female Citizens can build Attributes.
  • Female Citizens can build Hero Abilities.
PvP Arena There is a PvP Arena which can be reached from a portal in Outland. It allows two teams to fight each other for gold, lumber and fame. Enter the switches and wait until the fight starts. If you leave the arena or get killed before the fight ends, you will not win. Only the surviving members of the winning team will be winners.
