  • 70: AI for yourself.

Known Issues Unfortunately, this map has some known unfixed issues. If you think you know the cause of an issue or the solution to fix it, please report it on Discord.
  • Long loading time leads to errors for some players.
  • Enchanting does not work for all item abilities due to Warcraft bugs.
  • Goblin Town Hall has a weird light bug pointing to the hero glow.
  • Line of Sight Blockers do not work for flying units (occlusion height).
  • Researches increasing unit maximum life can lead to negative maximum life and instant unit deaths.
  • Release 2.0 of Warcraft III broke older version before 3.31 with a new frame hierarchy.
  • AI might crash in start locations which have too little ground area space.
  • Loading save games might crash because of string literals longer than 1023 characters.
Game Modes You can choose one of two game modes after the hero selection. As a warlord you play partly in the strategy mode and can build a base. This has the advantage that you have mostly a bigger army than other players. On the other hand, as a freelancer your hero starts with +15 points on all attributes and your hero gets 30% more experience from killing hostile units. Freelancers can see neutral buildings from start.
  • Warlord: Partly strategy mode.
  • Freelancer: Pure RPG mode, begins with +15 attributes and +30 % XP rate.
Players Players own heroes, units, buildings and resources. The number of players is determined in the game lobby. Computer players play with Warcraft's AI. Empty slots will not be playing. Users can leave at any time during a game. Users can also be kicked from games by votes.One single player is the host.The ownership of units can be transfered to other players using spells like Charm or chat commands. Players can use their save codes to to save/load data during a game. There are several fixed Computer players which cannot be changed in the game lobby. Alliances between players are preset by their game lobby team. Alliances can be changed during the game using chat commands.
  • "-players": Lists all players with their names, numbers, teams and colors.
  • X in chat commands for players can be their number, name or color name.
  • "-accounts": Lists all known accounts.
  • "-bans": Lists all banned account names.
  • "-votekick X": Starts a votekick against player X.
  • Fixed Computer player Gaia allows rescuing units.
  • Player The Burning Legion plays with Demon AI at Archimonde's realm.
  • Player Bosses owns all bosses and is allied with all creeps.
  • Player Neutral Hostile owns all creeps and is allied with all bosses.
  • Player Neutral Passive owns all NPCs and neutral buildings.
Heroes You have to pick a hero in the beginning of the game which can be repicked. You are allowed to pick two additional heroes at some point when you reached a higher level with your first hero.
  • "-repick": Repick your hero (you lose all units!).
  • "-fullrepick": Lose 3/4 of your XP but repick the game mode.
  • "-h1/-h2/-h3": to (re)pick hero 1/2/3 without losing all units.
  • Maximum level is 75.
  • Additional, bonus and boss heroes require a higher hero level of your first hero.
  • Hero 2 and 3 have no backpack.
  • Hero 2 and 3 have no backpack and no equipment bags.
  • Available at hero level 40.
Professions After choosing your race or game mode, you have to select a profession. The profession allows you to craft items. These items might help your hero or units to become stronger or to restore their stats or to fortify your base. Crafting items costs mana.
  • Craft items.
  • "-p1/-p2/-p3": (Re)pick your professions 1/2/3.
  • Additional professions require a higher hero level of your first hero.
  • Higher hero levels reduce the crafting cooldowns and increases crafted charges.
Races This selection is restricted to warlords. They receive a scepter and tiny town hall of their race which allows them to summon two workers and therefore to build their first base.
  • For Warlords only.
  • "-r1/-r2/-r3": (Re)pick your races 1/2/3.
  • Additional races require a higher hero level of your first hero.
Start Locations The start locations allow you to start the game at some specified area on the map. Some start locations may be occupied by other players including Warlord AI. The Clan Island is restricted to clan members.
Victory Every player can win the game by killing the boss Archimonde if the victory conditions are enabled. The game ends after this. All players who are not allied with the killing player will lose the game.
  • "-victory"/"-novictory": Enables/disables victory conditions.
